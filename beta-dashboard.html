Re-imagining

Beta Program Analytics Dashboard

Centralized reporting for a suite of beta apps — NPS and satisfaction trends, top issues, verbatim synthesis, focus group findings, war room logs, and version soak time recommendations.

Problem

▼

Across a portfolio of apps in beta — a news feed, a messaging platform, a groups app, a video app, and a social music experience — feedback was being collected but not synthesized in a way that made it actionable. NPS and satisfaction data arrived as raw exports. Product teams needed one place that consolidated bug reports, focus group findings, war room outcomes, feature requests, and customer verbatims — and surfaced what all of it meant for the next cycle.

Product Ownership

▼

Beta Release Management

Managed internal and external beta releases across five apps. Coordinated release timing, tester cohort selection, and go/no-go decisions based on soak time data and incoming feedback volume and severity.

Feedback Synthesis

Compiled NPS, CSAT, in-app verbatims, app store reviews, focus group transcripts, and war room notes into structured cycle reports. Identified trends, flagged regressions, and mapped every data source to a product decision.

Focus Group & War Room Leadership

Ran focus group sessions with segmented tester cohorts, facilitated usability task walkthroughs, and documented key findings. Led war room sessions during critical beta milestones — logging escalations, driving decisions, and assigning action items in real time.

Score Movement Analysis

For each cycle, modeled what fixing specific issues would move the score by — based on mention frequency across verbatims, focus group observations, and war room escalations. "Fix bugs 1 and 3 and you'd likely move NPS +6 points" became how product prioritized its bug backlog.

Tradeoffs

▼

Score movement model is directional, not predictive

"Fix bugs 1 and 3 and you'd likely move NPS +6 points" is a useful framing for prioritization conversations, but it's pattern-based, not a statistically rigorous forecast — teams needed to use it as a guide, not a promise.

War room log quality

Traceability of decisions is only as good as what gets recorded in the room. Rushed sessions with high-pressure decisions still produced sparse logs, limiting how useful the record was for retrospectives.

Soak time recommendations are judgment calls

The dashboard surfaces crash rates, severity trends, feedback volume — but the go/no-go decision still required human interpretation. The tool informed the call; it didn't make it.

Outcome

▼

Beta reports became a standing agenda item in sprint reviews across all five apps. The score movement framing became the standard way product managers prioritized bug fixes against feature work each cycle. War room decisions were formalized and traceable for the first time. Soak time recommendations were adopted as the gate criteria for promoting builds, measurably reducing critical bugs reaching external beta.
