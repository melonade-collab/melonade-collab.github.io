Re-imagining

Beta Program Analytics Dashboard

Centralized reporting for a suite of beta apps — tracking NPS and satisfaction trends, surfacing top bugs and feature requests, and quantifying what it would take to move the scores.

Problem

▼

Across a portfolio of apps in beta — a news feed, a messaging platform, a groups app, a video app, and a social music experience — feedback was being collected but not synthesized in a way that made it actionable. NPS and satisfaction data arrived as raw exports. Bug reports came through separate QA channels. Feature requests were scattered across feedback forms and app store reviews. There was no single view showing how a version was performing or whether trends were improving between releases.

Product teams needed more than a data dump. They needed to know: is this version better than the last one? What's driving the score? What would need to change to move it? And how long should the current version stay in soak before promoting to the next release gate?

Product Ownership

▼

Beta Release Management

Managed internal and external beta releases across five apps. Coordinated release timing, tester cohort selection, and go/no-go decisions based on soak time data and incoming feedback volume and severity.

Feedback Synthesis

Compiled NPS, CSAT, bug reports, and feature requests into structured reports for each version. Identified trends across releases, flagged regressions, and surfaced the feedback most likely to influence score movement if addressed.

Insight Framing

Translated raw metrics into product-actionable language — not just "NPS dropped 4 points" but "NPS dropped 4 points; crash on video resume is the single most-cited issue in 1-3 star verbatims, accounting for 38% of negative mentions." That's what product needed to act.

Score Movement Analysis

For each cycle, estimated what fixing top issues would move the score by — modeling impact based on mention frequency and sentiment weight. This gave product teams a prioritization frame: "Fix bugs 1 and 3 and you'd likely move NPS +6 points next cycle."

Outcome

▼

Beta reports became a standing agenda item in sprint reviews across the app portfolio. The score movement framing — "here's what would move NPS by X points" — became the standard way product managers prioritized bug fixes against feature work in each release cycle. Soak time recommendations were consistently adopted as the gate criteria for promoting builds, reducing the rate of critical bugs reaching external beta.
