Re-imagining

Consumer Shipping — Mobile

A mobile-first reimagining of the consumer shipping experience: ship a package or track existing shipments — no account required, no tracking number to retype.

Problem

▼

Consumers who shipped occasionally had no easy way to ship or track without a business account — and the carrier had no mobile app to fill that gap. The web experience required retyping tracking numbers every visit, since there were no consumer accounts to persist history. Confusion at drop-off points was common because customers had no clear guidance on where to go or what to bring.

Constraints

▼
  • No consumer account system — tracking history had to be stored locally
  • No native app — prototype scoped to a mobile web experience
  • Barcode/QR scanning requires device camera access (simulated here)
  • Tracking data had to work without an authenticated session
  • Needed to support both first-time shippers and repeat trackers in a single entry point

Product Ownership

▼

Flows

Designed two distinct flows — Ship and Track — unified under a single home screen, so the experience felt like one coherent app rather than two bolted-together tools.

Interaction Logic

Built a page-by-page step model for shipping (vs. the web's single-page disclosure) to suit thumb-native mobile navigation with a visible progress indicator throughout.

Service Design

Defined local persistence for tracking numbers so consumers could return without re-entering — solving the biggest tracking friction without needing backend accounts.

Platform Change

Scoped the scan-to-track feature as a camera-permission flow, bridging the gap between physical package labels and digital tracking without requiring manual entry.

How It Worked

▼

The home screen offered two actions: Ship and Track. The Ship flow walked users through address entry, package selection, a quote, and payment — one step per screen, with a progress indicator. The Track screen displayed previously added tracking numbers with live status and a progress bar showing each shipment's stage in the shipping system. Users could add new tracking numbers by typing or scanning a barcode/QR code, which were then saved locally for future visits.

Outcome

▼

Demonstrated that a no-account consumer shipping and tracking experience was viable on mobile. The scan-to-track capability reduced the primary friction point of tracking (retyping long tracking numbers), and the unified Ship + Track home screen established a clear mental model for the app — matching how consumers actually think about shipping: I need to send something, or I need to find something I sent.

Frequently asked questions
How do I track my shipment? ▼
Use the Track tab at the bottom. Add a tracking number by typing it or scanning the barcode on your shipping label.
My tracking status looks wrong. ▼
Status updates can take a few hours, especially during high volume. If it hasn't resolved in 24 hours, call us with your tracking number.
Can I change a delivery address? ▼
Address changes may be possible if the package hasn't been picked up yet. Call us as soon as possible with your tracking number.
How do I get a refund? ▼
If your shipment was lost or significantly delayed, you may be eligible for a refund. Contact us by phone with your tracking number and order details.
What items can't I ship? ▼
Prohibited items include hazardous materials, perishable goods, live animals, and firearms. Contact us if you're unsure about a specific item.
