Prototype · Agentic AI

NL Shipping Form

Shipping forms ask structured questions in a domain most people aren't fluent in. This prototype explores a natural language intake where you describe the shipment and the agent handles the translation to form fields.

Problem

▼

Shipping forms require structured inputs — weight, dimensions, service tier, declared value, special handling — before you've even described what you're sending. Most people who ship infrequently don't think in those terms, and the form shouldn't make that their problem to solve first.

Tech

▼
  • Client-side NLP parser — extracts origin, destination, weight, dimensions, service type, declared value, contents, and special handling flags from free-text input
  • Longest-match city lookup across 80+ Canadian cities and regions
  • Service inference from delivery dates — calculates business days to recommend the right service tier when none is stated
  • Shipment type detection with contextual packaging recommendations (parcels, envelopes, legal envelopes)
  • Animated multi-step processing pipeline

Features

▼
  • Natural language input — describe your shipment the way you'd explain it to someone; the agent handles the translation to form fields
  • Animated parsing pipeline — shows the agent working through your description in real time across four steps
  • Smart field fill — AI-extracted values populate with an amber "AI" badge; missing fields get a dashed border so you know exactly what needs your input
  • Parcel type recommendation — detects document shipments and recommends the right envelope size, including where to pick one up for free
  • Service inference — if you mention a delivery deadline but no service tier, it works backwards from the date to pick the right level
  • Rate comparison — generates Ground, Express, and Overnight quotes once the form is complete
  • Example prompts — quick-load scenarios for fragile electronics, palletized freight, and legal documents

Why This

▼

Every shipping form is a domain knowledge test — service tiers, dimensional weight, declared value — terminology that infrequent shippers don't use or think in. This prototype puts the translation work on the agent, client-side, so the interface can start where people actually are: a plain description of what they need to send.
