Prototype · Live API + AI

Plant Care Tracker

Static plant care cards tell you when to water in a vacuum. This prototype connects your care schedule to live conditions — weather, humidity, UV — and gives advice that's specific to today.

The Problem

▼

Generic care schedules don't account for what's actually happening in your home or outside your window. The same "water every 10 days" rule means something different on a hot, sunny July afternoon than on a grey, overcast November morning. A static card can't make that distinction.

Tech

▼
  • Live weather via the Open-Meteo API — temperature, weather code, humidity, UV index, and wind speed
  • Watering history persisted to localStorage with interval tracking
  • Contextual AI insight engine — cross-references watering status with live weather to generate plant-specific advice
  • City selection from user profile, with support for 23 Canadian cities
  • Heating season awareness — adjusts advice based on time of year and indoor heating likelihood

Features

▼
  • Plant collection — manage multiple plants, add new ones, and see watering status at a glance for each
  • Care cards — per-plant light, water frequency, and humidity requirements
  • Watering tracker — logs each watering, shows days since last, next ETA, and overdue status with a progress bar
  • Live weather — fetches current conditions for your city: temperature, description, humidity, and UV index
  • AI insight card — generates contextual watering advice by combining your history with today's weather: sunny means faster drying, overcast means wait longer, rain means collect some for your plants
  • Heating season awareness — in cooler months, flags that indoor heating dries out the air and recommends humidity support
  • User profile — set your name and city; greets you by name and uses your location for weather

Why This

▼

Every plant care app gives you a schedule. None of them know what the weather is doing outside your window. This prototype connects the two — watering history plus live conditions — so the advice you get is grounded in what's actually happening today, not a rule of thumb written for average conditions that rarely apply.
