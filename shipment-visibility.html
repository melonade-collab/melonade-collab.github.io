Re-imagining

Shipment Visibility Web App

A self-serve web app giving medium and high-volume business shippers real-time visibility into their entire shipment portfolio — without waiting for a manually-built Excel report.

Problem

▼

Business shippers had no portfolio-level view — the portal only supported individual lookups, so operations teams manually built Excel reports that took hours each week and were stale by the time they landed. Exception management was the sharpest pain point: a stuck package could sit undetected for days before a customer called to complain.

Constraints

▼
  • Shipment event data was batch-processed every 15 minutes — no real-time event stream existed
  • Internal reporting teams needed to remain involved for ad hoc reporting requests
  • No export pipeline existed — reports had been entirely manual up to this point
  • Session authentication already existed for business accounts, so the authenticated context was a given

Product Ownership

▼

Discovery

Interviewed six high-volume shippers to understand how they tracked their portfolios. Found that most used a combination of Excel exports, Slack pings to their CSM, and individual lookups — with exception follow-up typically delayed until the next morning's report.

Information Architecture

Designed a five-stage status taxonomy (Label Created → Picked Up → In Transit → Out for Delivery → Delivered, plus Exceptions) that aligned the internal scan codes to customer mental models and could power both the chart and the list view.

Prototype & Validation

Built and tested a clickable prototype with three shippers in 60-minute sessions. Key finding: exception prioritization by days-since-last-scan was more useful than sorting by tracking number.

Report Design

Defined four standard reports in collaboration with the internal ops team — Activity Summary, Exception Report, Delivery Performance, and Volume by Destination — reducing manual report generation by approximately six hours per week.

How It Worked

▼

After logging in, authenticated business shippers landed on a dashboard showing the last 30 days of shipment activity. The status distribution donut chart and horizontal bar chart gave an at-a-glance view of portfolio health — how many packages were at each stage, and whether any were in exception.

Clicking any segment in the chart, or any status tab in the Shipments view, filtered the list to that group. Clicking a tracking number expanded full shipment details — including from and to addresses, since the user was already authenticated and permissions were confirmed at session start. The Exceptions tab surfaced stuck packages with a reason code and days since last scan, so teams could prioritize follow-up without waiting for a report.

Two additional features were built alongside the core dashboard: exception alerts (email and SMS notifications when a shipment entered an exception state) and scheduled report delivery (daily or weekly summaries sent automatically to configured recipients).

Outcome

▼

Piloted with 12 medium-volume accounts over six weeks. Manual report generation dropped from approximately six hours per week to on-demand and self-serve. Exception response time improved from next-business-day — when the daily Excel report was circulated — to same-session.
