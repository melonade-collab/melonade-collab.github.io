Re-imagining

Roaming & Long Distance Rates

A conversational lookup tool embedded in the carrier's virtual assistant — letting customers check international rates by country, compare pay-per-use versus packages, and add a travel add-on without leaving the chat.

Problem

▼

Customers planning international travel wanted a quick, confident answer to a simple question: what will my phone cost abroad, and is there a better option? The decision — pay-per-use versus a travel package — had real financial stakes, and customers needed enough context to make it without having to call in.

The opportunity was to surface that information conversationally, within the virtual assistant, at the moment customers were already reaching out — and to make adding a package a natural next step rather than a separate journey.

Constraints

▼
  • Package purchase couldn't be completed inline at launch — the add-on had to hand off to a checkout flow with the customer's selection pre-loaded
  • Roaming rates and long distance rates had different structures and needed to be normalized into a single, coherent response
  • Customers asking about rates mid-trip needed pay-per-use information surfaced prominently, not just package upsell — the response logic had to account for both scenarios
  • Package availability varied depending on where the customer was in their billing cycle — the tool had to reflect what was actually purchasable at the time of the query
  • Input handling needed to cover country name variants, abbreviations, and common misspellings without requiring exact matches

Product Ownership

▼

Intent Design

Designed the conversational flow covering direct country queries ("roaming in France"), comparison questions ("is it cheaper to buy a package?"), and mid-trip inquiries. Mapped edge cases: no coverage countries, countries with only data roaming, and requests about specific service types.

Rate Display Logic

Defined how rates were displayed — voice per minute, text per message, data per day or per MB — and how package savings were calculated and surfaced relative to estimated pay-per-use costs based on a 7-day average trip assumption.

Package Add-On Flow

Designed the handoff from lookup result to package purchase: contextual package recommendations based on the country queried, savings highlight vs. per-use rates, and a pre-populated cart handoff to the transactional flow with the customer's selection pre-loaded.

Cross-Team Alignment

Coordinated across product, billing, and platform teams to define the rate display logic, package availability rules, and checkout handoff behaviour. Managed scope negotiation when the inline purchase capability shifted to a handoff model for launch.

Outcome

▼

International rate inquiries became one of the highest-volume handled intents in the virtual assistant within the first quarter. Call deflection for rate lookups improved measurably. Package attachment rate through the chatbot flow was strong — attributed to contextual comparison and the proximity of the purchase option within the same conversation.
